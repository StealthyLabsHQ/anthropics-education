# AI Patterns by Academic Discipline

> **When to load this file:** when the text to humanize comes from a specific discipline and requires detection of domain-specific patterns. The generic vocabulary list is not enough - every field has its own AI writing markers.

---

## 1. Accounting / Management / Finance

### Vocabulary - AI patterns in accounting

| AI expression | Why it is a signal | Natural alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "tax optimization" | Too vague, pure buzzword. No mechanism specified. | "reducing the tax burden by choosing the flat-rate tax regime" (specify the mechanism) |
| "performance management" | Everyone says this, it distinguishes nothing. | "monthly tracking of budget variances" (anchor on the concrete tool) |
| "value creation" | Management cliché adopted without reflection. | "12% increase in net profit over the financial year" (quantify) |
| "decision-support tool" | Generic, describes no real tool. | "Excel dashboard with cash flow indicators" |
| "competitiveness lever" | Consultant-speak, not a student's vocabulary. | "advantage on financial statement production timelines" |
| "accompany the transformation" | Meaningless concretely. | "train the 3 staff on Xero in 2 weeks" |
| "in a context of globalization" | Irrelevant for an 8-person firm or a local SME. | Delete entirely. |
| "business process" | Acceptable but AI overuses it. | "invoice approval workflow" |
| "digitalization" | OK if used precisely, otherwise hollow. | "switching from paper invoices to electronic format via the government portal" |
| "corporate governance" | Often misused, applied without verification. | Verify if the context truly involves governance (board, shareholders). |
| "stakeholders" | Too vague, hides lack of field knowledge. | Name them: "the clients, the accountant, and the statutory auditor" |
| "optimal resource management" | Means nothing without data. | Describe what was done with numbers: "15-day reduction in working capital by renegotiating supplier payment terms" |
| "strategic outsourcing" | Pompous and hollow. | "outsourcing payroll to a specialist provider" |
| "operational synergy" | Pure buzzword, nobody talks like this in a firm. | "the two teams share the same client file" |
| "competitive benchmarking" | Unnecessary jargon. | "comparison with 3 similarly-sized firms" |
| "maximize profitability" | Hollow formula without grounding. | "increase the margin rate from 22% to 27% by reducing subcontracting costs" |

### Structure - AI patterns in accounting

- **The perfectly balanced 3-part plan.** AI loves writing "Part 1: Theoretical Framework / Part 2: Practical Framework / Part 3: Recommendations" with equally long sections. In a real thesis, Part 2 (fieldwork, case study) is almost always longer than the others. The imbalance is normal.
- **Generic conclusions about digitalization.** AI systematically ends accounting theses with "digitalization is transforming the profession" and "the future will be shaped by artificial intelligence." A real student concludes on their specific case: what they observed, what worked, what did not work.
- **Absence of quantitative data in the theoretical framework.** Even in the first section, a real accounting student cites statistics (number of firms, average revenue, sector digitalization rates). AI stays in abstraction.

### Tone

- Real accounting theses use numbers constantly. If an entire paragraph contains no figure, it is probably AI.
- Real students cite technical standards by article number (e.g. "in accordance with ASC 606"), not just "the accounting standard."
- The technical vocabulary is precise but not pompous: a student writes "adjusting journal entries," not "normative accounting adjustment procedures."

## 2. Law

### Vocabulary - AI patterns in law

| AI expression | Why it is a signal | Natural alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "within the current legal framework" | Which framework? Which text? Which date? | Specify: "under the Consumer Rights Act 2015" |
| "in accordance with legislation in force" | AI never cites the specific article. | Cite: "pursuant to s.14 of the Sale of Goods Act 1979" |
| "settled case law" | Which? Since when? | Name the case: "since Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] AC 562" |
| "enhanced protection of rights" | Which rights? For whom? Which text? | "strengthening of the right to be forgotten under the GDPR (Art. 17)" |
| "the evolution of law" | When? Which reform? Which movement? | "the Consumer Protection Act 2015 reform" |
| "the fundamental principles of law" | Which ones exactly? | Name them: "the principle of legality (nulla poena sine lege)" |
| "the legislator intended" | AI invents the legislator's intention. | Cite preparatory work or parliamentary debates. |
| "it should be noted that" | AI language tic, not legal. | Delete and go straight to the point. |
| "legal certainty" | Often used as a catch-all. | Specify the issue: "the foreseeability of the outcome for the contracting party" |
| "a balance between rights" | AI loves the balance metaphor without problematizing it. | Identify the precise conflict and the hierarchy retained by the judge. |

### Structure - AI patterns in law

- **AI forgets the legal syllogism.** Legal reasoning follows a strict schema: facts, legal characterization, applicable rule, solution. AI often skips characterization and jumps straight from describing the facts to the conclusion.
- **AI does not distinguish between positive law and doctrine.** A real law student knows there is a difference between what the statute says, what the judge says, and what academics say. AI blends them all into one fluent paragraph.
- **AI does not use footnotes correctly.** In law, every normative assertion must be sourced in a footnote. AI integrates references into the body text or omits them.
- **AI produces two-part / two-subsection plans but without genuine dialectic.** The apparent plan is there, but the subsections do not really oppose each other.

### Tone

- Legal English has its own register with specific formulas: "the court held," "obiter dicta," "ratio decidendi," "per Lord X." AI uses them sometimes, but inconsistently or anachronistically.
- Do not over-simplify legal language - it is a legitimate technical register. But do not let AI make it pompous either.
- A real law student uses specific transitional phrases: "conversely," "by contrast," "the Court of Appeal, however, held that..." AI prefers "furthermore," "additionally," "it is important to note."

## 3. Social Sciences / Psychology

### Vocabulary - AI patterns in social sciences

| AI expression | Why it is a signal | Natural alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "social dynamics" | What precise mechanism? | Name it: "social reproduction (Bourdieu, 1970)," "distinction strategies" |
| "social fabric" | Worn metaphor, describes nothing. | Describe the reality: "the neighborhood," "the peer group," "Year 10 at school X" |
| "social actors" | Who exactly? | "the teachers at the vocational secondary school," "the youth justice officers" |
| "identity construction" | Often misused, applied without theoretical framework. | Specify the framework: "identity construction in the sense of Kaufmann (2004)" or "narrative identity in Ricoeur" |
| "comprehensive approach" | OK but AI uses it without explaining the methodological implications. | Explain: "a comprehensive approach in the Weberian sense, aimed at understanding the meaning actors give to their practices" |
| "the field" | Acceptable but must be specified. | "the study conducted with 12 nurses at hospital X between March and June 2025" |
| "societal issue" | Catch-all. | Name the problem: "the increasing rate of school dropout in disadvantaged areas" |
| "living together" | Political catch-all, not scientific. | Use a precise sociological concept or delete. |
| "social bond" | Overused without definition. | Specify whose sense: "organic solidarity (Durkheim)" or describe concretely. |
| "impact on society" | As vague as it gets. | Describe the precise effect on a specific group with data. |
| "resilience" | Catch-all term since the pandemic. | Use in its precise psychological sense (Cyrulnik) or replace with a description. |

### Structure - AI patterns in social sciences

- **AI forgets epistemological positioning.** In social sciences, you must explain where you are speaking from: constructivism, positivism, interactionism, etc. AI produces text that floats without epistemological grounding.
- **AI does not discuss reflexivity.** A real social sciences researcher questions their own position: their relationship to the field, their biases, their involvement. AI never mentions this.
- **In psychology, AI does not cite the scales or instruments used.** A real psychology thesis mentions the BDI-II, the WISC-V, a 7-point Likert scale - not just "a questionnaire was administered."
- **AI does not distinguish levels of analysis.** Micro (individual), meso (group/institution), macro (society): a real social sciences thesis navigates between these levels. AI usually stays at the macro level.

### Tone

- Writing in social sciences is more personal than in hard sciences. "We" is expected, and sometimes "I" (especially in qualitative sociology or education sciences).
- Real social sciences students quote long passages and discuss them word by word. AI summarizes without genuinely engaging with the text.
- The tone is analytical but not cold. A social sciences student can write "this situation surprised me during the interview" - AI never will.

## 4. Computing / Information Systems

### Vocabulary - AI patterns in computing

| AI expression | Why it is a signal | Natural alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "robust and scalable architecture" | Two marketing adjectives, no substance. | Name the technologies: "REST architecture with React on the front end, Node.js/Express on the back end, PostgreSQL for the database" |
| "innovative solution" | In what way? Compared to what? | Describe the difference: "unlike the existing tool which required manual input, the application automatically parses PDFs" |
| "performance optimization" | What response time? What benchmark? | "load time reduced from 3.2s to 0.8s by adding an index on the orders table" |
| "modern technology stack" | "Modern" means nothing. | Name the tools with their versions: "React 18.2, TypeScript 5.0, Docker 24.0" |
| "production deployment" | How? On what? | "deployment on a VPS via Docker Compose with a GitLab CI pipeline" |
| "development best practices" | Which ones? | "unit tests with Jest (73% coverage), systematic code review via merge requests" |
| "intuitive user interface" | Unsubstantiated value judgment. | "interface tested with 5 users, registration flow completion rate: 92%" |
| "secure data management" | What mechanisms? | "password hashing with bcrypt, JWT tokens with 24-hour expiry" |
| "agile methodology" | Often applied without reality. | "2-week sprints with daily stand-up, backlog managed on Jira" (or admit it was informal) |
| "system interoperability" | Hollow buzzword without detail. | "communication via REST API with OAuth 2.0 authentication" |

### Structure - AI patterns in computing

- **AI forgets the diagrams.** A real computing thesis contains UML diagrams (use cases, classes, sequences), architecture schemas, flowcharts. AI produces text without visual support.
- **Code snippets are generic.** AI puts code examples that look like official documentation, not the real project code (with its specific variable names, its comments, its imperfections).
- **AI does not mention versions, configurations, or errors encountered.** A real developer writes "I had a CORS error that I resolved by configuring the cors middleware with the credentials: true option." AI writes "the system works correctly."
- **AI does not talk about technical debt or trade-offs.** A real project has questionable choices made for lack of time - AI presents everything as optimal.

### Tone

- Technical theses alternate between a formal register (theoretical framework, state of the art) and an informal technical register (implementation description). AI maintains a uniform tone.
- Real development students mention bugs, approaches that failed, workarounds. AI only presents the success path.
- Technical English vocabulary is normal and expected in computing (framework, endpoint, middleware). AI sometimes translates these unnecessarily.

## 5. Health / Biomedical Sciences

### Vocabulary - AI patterns in health

| AI expression | Why it is a signal | Natural alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "holistic patient care" | What protocol? What condition? What team? | "care of the type 2 diabetic patient following the 2023 clinical guideline" |
| "multidisciplinary approach" | Which professionals exactly? | "collaboration between the coordinating nurse, the endocrinologist, and the dietitian" |
| "therapeutic patient education" | What programme? Validated by whom? | "TPE programme validated by the regional health authority, 4 sessions of 2 hours on blood glucose self-monitoring" |
| "evidence-based medicine" | What level of evidence? Which meta-analysis? | "Grade A recommendation based on the 2022 Cochrane meta-analysis (n = 3,400)" |
| "improve quality of care" | Measured how? | "30-day readmission rate reduced from 18% to 11%" |
| "patient at the center" | A slogan, not a described practice. | Describe concretely: "collecting the patient's preferences via a semi-structured interview before the therapeutic decision" |
| "holistic approach" | Rarely defined, often hollow. | Specify the dimensions: "taking into account the somatic, psychological, and social dimensions (Engel's biopsychosocial model)" |
| "coordinated care pathway" | Which pathway, between which actors? | "town-hospital pathway with handoff via the shared medical record and letter to the GP within 48 hours" |

### Structure - AI patterns in health

- **AI forgets the IMRAD structure.** Biomedical science theses follow Introduction, Method, Results, And Discussion. AI often produces a thematic plan instead of a methodological one.
- **AI does not mention ethical considerations.** A real health thesis describes ethics committee approval, data protection registration, and informed consent collection. AI skips this step.
- **AI does not discuss sample limitations.** Size, selection bias, response rate, missing data - a real health student knows that their cohort of 28 patients has limitations.
- **AI does not detail materials and methods.** A real thesis specifies the study type (retrospective cohort, randomized controlled trial, qualitative interview study), inclusion/exclusion criteria, and statistical tools used (chi-square test, logistic regression, p < 0.05).

### Tone

- The tone in health sciences is factual and measured. Real students write "the results suggest" and not "the results prove."
- Caution is warranted: "this trend could be explained by..." rather than "this demonstrates that..."
- Abbreviations are everywhere and defined at first occurrence. AI often forgets to define them or uses them inconsistently.

## 6. Literature / Languages / Philosophy

### Specific patterns - Literature and languages

- **AI gives surface-level analyses without close reading.** A real literature student stops at a word, a phrasing, a rhetorical figure. AI summarizes the passage without examining its texture.
- **AI quotes without discussing lexical, syntactic, or stylistic choices.** Quoting a passage is just the start. Analyzing it word by word is the work. AI does the first, not the second.
- **AI does not distinguish narrator and author.** Classic error: attributing to Austen what the narrator of *Pride and Prejudice* says.
- **AI does not situate the work in its production context.** Writing conditions, critical reception, edition history - all of this is missing.

### Specific patterns - Philosophy

- **AI does not engage enough with counter-arguments.** Real philosophical work takes objections seriously. AI mentions them in one sentence before dismissing them.
- **AI summarizes a philosopher's thought without problematizing it.** Expounding what Kant says is not philosophizing. Showing why his position is problematic, why it is in tension with another, is philosophizing.
- **AI uses conceptual shortcuts.** "Descartes thinks body and mind are separate" - this is a caricature. A real student nuances, contextualizes, quotes the text.
- **AI does not do conceptual work.** In philosophy, you define, distinguish, redefine. AI takes concepts for granted.

### Tone

- In literature, style matters. The writing must be careful, sometimes literary. AI produces functional text without writing quality.
- In philosophy, clarity comes first. No unnecessary jargon, but extreme conceptual precision. AI does the inverse: jargon without precision.

## Summary Table - Alert signals by discipline

| Discipline | Signal #1 | Signal #2 | Signal #3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Accounting / Management** | Entire paragraph without any figure | Buzzwords not anchored in a concrete case | Generic conclusion about "the digital future" |
| **Law** | No precise reference to a statute or case | No legal syllogism reasoning | Legal vocabulary too simplified or inconsistent |
| **Social Sciences / Psychology** | No epistemological positioning | Theoretical concepts used without sourcing | No trace of researcher reflexivity |
| **Computing** | No diagram, code, or screenshot | Technologies described without being named or versioned | No mention of failure, bug, or technical compromise |
| **Health** | No ethical considerations (ethics committee, data protection, consent) | No level of evidence or reference to guidelines | IMRAD structure not followed |
| **Literature / Philosophy** | Quotation followed by summary instead of word-by-word analysis | No close reading or textual work | A thinker's ideas summarized without being problematized |

## How to Use This File

1. **Identify the discipline** of the text to humanize.
2. **Check vocabulary patterns**: does the text contain the expressions listed above? If so, replace them with the concrete alternatives.
3. **Check structural patterns**: does the plan, transitions, and organization match the discipline's expectations?
4. **Check the tone**: is the register what a real student in this discipline would use?
5. **Cross-reference with the summary table**: if two of the three signals are present, the text probably needs substantial work, not just word replacement.
