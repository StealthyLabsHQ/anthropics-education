## 1. APA 7th edition

Most widely used in France for social sciences, management, psychology,
and education sciences. Often the default when the institution specifies nothing.

### In-text

| Situation | Format | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 1 author | (Last name, year) | (Smith, 2022) |
| 2 authors | (Last name & Last name, year) | (Smith & Johnson, 2021) |
| 3+ authors | (First author et al., year) | (Smith et al., 2020) |
| Direct quotation | (Last name, year, p. X) | (Smith, 2022, p. 45) |
| Author in the sentence | Last name (year) | Smith (2022) argues that... |
| Secondary source | (Original author, cited in Author read, year) | (Weber, cited in Smith, 2022) |
| Organization | (Full name, year) then (Abbreviation, year) | (World Health Organization [WHO], 2021) then (WHO, 2021) |

### In the bibliography

**Book:**
Smith, J. (2022). *Book title in italics*. Publisher.

**Book with multiple authors:**
Smith, J., Johnson, P., & Brown, A. (2021). *Book title*. Publisher.

**Chapter in a collected volume:**
Smith, J. (2022). Chapter title. In P. Johnson (Ed.), *Volume title* (pp. 45-67). Publisher.

**Journal article:**
Smith, J., & Johnson, P. (2023). Article title. *Journal Name*, 49(2), 112-130. https://doi.org/10.xxxx

**Online article without DOI:**
Smith, J. (2023). Article title. *Website name*. https://www.example.com/article

**Institutional report:**
World Health Organization. (2023). *Report title*. https://www.who.int/report

**Thesis or dissertation:**
Smith, J. (2023). *Thesis title* [Master's dissertation, University Name]. Repository. https://repository.example.edu/xxxxx

**Webpage:**
Smith, J. (2023, March 15). Page title. *Website name*. https://www.example.com/page

**Legislation:**
[Country-specific format] Act/Law No. XXXX of [date] relating to [subject]. Official Journal.

### Sorting rules

- Alphabetical order by first author's last name
- Multiple works by the same author: chronological order (2020 before 2023)
- Same author, same year: add a, b, c after the year (2022a, 2022b)
- Hanging indent: 1.27 cm from the 2nd line

## 2. French citation standard (footnotes)

Used in law, history, and sometimes accounting/management in certain
French universities. References appear in footnotes, not in the text.

*Note: this is a France-specific citation standard. International alternatives for these fields include Chicago Notes-Bibliography for humanities and law, and APA 7 for management.*

### First occurrence (full note)

**Book:**
¹ DUPONT (Jean), *Book title*, Paris, Dunod, 2022, p. 45.

**Article:**
² DUPONT (Jean), "Article title", *Revue française de gestion*, no. 2, 2023, p. 112-130.

**Thesis:**
³ DUPONT (Jean), *Thesis title*, doctoral thesis, University Paris 1, 2023, p. 78.

### Subsequent occurrences (abbreviations)

| Abbreviation | Meaning | Usage |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| *Ibid.* | Ibidem (same source as previous note) | ⁴ *Ibid.*, p. 52. |
| *Op. cit.* | Opere citato (previously cited work) | ⁵ DUPONT (J.), *op. cit.*, p. 67. |
| *Loc. cit.* | Loco citato (same page as last citation of this source) | ⁶ DUPONT (J.), *loc. cit.* |
| *Cf.* | Confer (see, compare) | ⁷ *Cf.* MARTIN (P.), *op. cit.*, p. 23. |

### Final bibliography

Even when footnotes are used, a final bibliography is generally expected.
Sort by source type then alphabetically:

```
BOOKS
DUPONT (Jean), Book title, Paris, Dunod, 2022, 350 p.

ARTICLES
DUPONT (Jean), "Article title", Revue française de gestion, no. 2, 2023, p. 112-130.

LEGAL TEXTS
Act no. 2023-XXX of [date] relating to [subject].

ONLINE SOURCES
DUPONT (Jean), "Title", www.example.com, accessed DD/MM/YYYY.
```

## 3. Harvard

Close to APA but with some differences. Used in some business schools
and international programs.

### In-text

Identical to APA: (Last name, year) or (Last name, year, p. X)

### In the bibliography (differences from APA)

**Book:**
Smith, J., 2022. *Book title*. London: Publisher.

**Article:**
Smith, J. and Johnson, P., 2023. Article title. *Journal Name*, 49(2), pp.112-130.

Notable differences: year after the name (not in parentheses), place of publication
included, "pp." for article pages.

## 4. Vancouver (numerical)

Used in medicine, pharmacy, biomedical sciences. Sources are numbered
in order of appearance in the text.

### In-text

Number in brackets: [1], [2], [1,3], [1-4]

### In the bibliography

Sources listed in order of first appearance (not alphabetical):

1. Smith J, Johnson P. Article title. J Name. 2023;49(2):112-30.
2. Smith J. Book title. London: Publisher; 2022.

## 5. Chicago

Two variants: notes-bibliography (humanities) and author-date (sciences).
Sometimes required in international programs.

### Notes-bibliography (Variant A)

Footnotes + final bibliography. Similar to the French standard
but with Anglo-Saxon punctuation conventions.

¹ Jean Smith, *Book title* (London: Publisher, 2022), 45.

### Author-date (Variant B)

Similar to APA/Harvard: (Smith 2022, 45)

## 6. Common errors

| Error | Correction |
|--------|-----------|
| Citing Wikipedia as a primary source | Use the sources cited by Wikipedia, not Wikipedia itself |
| "According to the internet..." | Name the precise source (website, author, date) |
| No access date for websites | Add "accessed DD/MM/YYYY" or the retrieval URL |
| Mixing standards in the same document | Choose ONE standard and stick to it |
| Citing an author without having read them (secondary source) | Use "cited in" and read the original if possible |
| Bibliography with no correspondence to the text | Every source cited in the text must appear in the bibliography, and vice versa |
| Unintentional plagiarism (paraphrase too close to the original) | Rewrite AND cite the source: the citation protects against the accusation |
