# Discipline-Specific Guidelines

> **When to load this file:** when the student's discipline requires citation standards, methodologies, or resources that differ from the general approach. This file allows you to immediately adapt the register, recommended sources, and formal expectations to the relevant field.

---

## Accounting / Management / Finance

**Citation standard:** APA 7 or French citation standard depending on the institution.

**Specific expectations:**
- Draw on the PCG (French General Chart of Accounts) and the CGI (French General Tax Code).
- Support every argument with real, up-to-date figures.
- Use concrete business cases (case studies, annual reports, publicly available financial data).

**Key sources:**
- DALLOZ, Légifrance, Ordre des Experts-Comptables (French-specific resources)
- Revue Française de Comptabilité, Revue Française de Gestion, Finance Contrôle Stratégie

**Databases:**
- Cairn (management, French-specific), Xerfi (sector studies), INSEE

**AI vocabulary traps in accounting:**
- "tax optimization": too vague; replace with the specific tax mechanism targeted
- "performance management": empty buzzword without a quantified indicator
- "value creation": cliché to avoid unless tied to a calculation (EVA, ROI, etc.)
- Rule: always anchor with numerical examples and normative references.

**Key reference authors:** Colasse, Burlaud, Bouquin, Savall, Meyssonnier.

## Law

**Citation standard:** French citation standard (numbered footnotes, infra-page references).

**Specific expectations:**
- Case commentary: case summary sheet, structured analysis (meaning, value, scope).
- Strict adherence to the hierarchy of norms (constitutional block, statute, regulation, case law).
- Legal syllogism: major premise (rule of law) → minor premise (qualified facts) → conclusion.
- Rigorous distinction between fact and law.

**Key sources:**
- DALLOZ, Lexis360, Légifrance, JORF (French-specific resources)
- AJDA (administrative law), Recueil Lebon, RTD Civ (civil law)

**Methodology: legal practical case:**
1. Identification of relevant facts
2. Legal classification of facts
3. Identification of the applicable rule of law
4. Application of the rule to the facts (syllogism)
5. Reasoned conclusion

**Key reference authors:** Carbonnier, Terré, Malaurie, Cornu.

## Social Sciences and Humanities (SSH) / Psychology

**Citation standard:** APA 7 (near-universal in these disciplines in France).

**Specific expectations:**
- Explicit epistemological positioning (constructivism, positivism, interactionism, etc.).
- Researcher reflexivity: situate one's relationship to the field and the object of study.
- Qualitative fieldwork is often favored, but mixed approaches are increasingly common.

**Key sources:**
- Cairn, PsycINFO, Persée (French-specific for Cairn and Persée)
- Revue française de sociologie, L'Année sociologique

**Common methodologies:**
- Semi-structured interviews (interview guide, transcription, thematic analysis)
- Participant or non-participant observation
- Discourse analysis
- Ethnography

**Key reference authors (sociology):** Bourdieu, Durkheim, Weber, Lahire, Kaufmann.

**Key reference authors (psychology):** Piaget, Vygotsky, Bandura, Deci & Ryan.

## Computer Science / Information Systems

**Citation standard:** IEEE or APA depending on the institution.

**Specific expectations:**
- UML diagrams (use case, class, sequence, activity).
- Description of technical architecture (client-server, microservices, MVC, etc.).
- Documented and readable code, with relevant excerpts in the body of the thesis.
- Benchmarks and performance tests to justify technical choices.

**Key sources:**
- ACM Digital Library, IEEE Xplore, arXiv (CS section), Springer LNCS

**Thesis specifics:**
- Technical section expected with screenshots, architecture diagrams, commented code excerpts.
- Voluminous technical appendices accepted (full source code, configurations).

**Common tools:**
- LaTeX (for technical reports and formulas)
- Git (version control, mention in methodology if relevant)
- diagrams.net, draw.io, PlantUML (for diagrams)

**Methodologies:**
- Agile / Scrum (for development projects: sprints, backlog, retrospectives)
- Design Science Research (for information systems research: artifact, evaluation, contribution)

## Health / Biomedical Sciences

**Citation standard:** Vancouver (numbered in order of appearance in the text).

**Specific expectations:**
- Mandatory ethical protocol: ethics committee approval (CPP, French research ethics committee), CNIL declaration (French data protection authority).
- EBM (Evidence-Based Medicine) approach: level of evidence, grade of recommendation.
- Level of evidence clearly identified for every source cited.

**Key sources:**
- PubMed, HAS (French health authority, good practice recommendations), Cochrane Library, EM Consulte

**Specifics:**
- Structured abstract in IMRAD format (Introduction, Methods, Results, And Discussion).
- Systematic declaration of conflicts of interest.
- Results tables with statistical values (p, 95% CI, etc.).

**Key reference authors / bodies:** HAS, WHO, INSERM.

## Literature / Languages / Philosophy

**Citation standard:** often French citation standard (footnotes) or Chicago (author-date or notes-bibliography depending on the variant).

**Specific expectations:**
- Rigorous textual analysis: structured commentary, linear explanation.
- Philosophical essay: demanding problematization, dialectical or thematic progression.
- Argumentation grounded in precise quotations from the works studied.

**Key sources:**
- Persée, Fabula (literature, French-specific), PhilPapers, Cairn

**Citation specifics:**
- Long quotation (> 3 lines): indented, no quotation marks, single spacing.
- Short quotation: in quotation marks, integrated into the body text.
- Edition matters: always specify which edition of a work is used and which translation if applicable (translator, year, publisher).

## Education Sciences / Sports Sciences (STAPS)

**Citation standard:** APA 7.

**Specific expectations:**
- Explicit and consistent theory-practice link (articulate theoretical frameworks with field observations).
- Competency frameworks: draw on official frameworks (French Ministry of Higher Education, National Education, sports federations).
- Field experience is essential: placement, observation in a school or sports setting, data collection.

**Key sources:**
- Cairn, Revue EPS, STAPS (journal), Éducation et formations (French-specific resources)

**Sports Sciences (STAPS) specifics:**
- Physiological data (VO2max, heart rate, lactate levels, etc.) with detailed measurement protocols.
- Experimental protocols: control group, randomization, pre-test / post-test.
- Statistics: parametric and non-parametric tests, effect size, significance.
