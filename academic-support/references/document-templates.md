## 1. Cover page: Thesis / Internship report

```
[INSTITUTION LOGO]                              [COMPANY LOGO if applicable]

                    [INSTITUTION NAME]
              [Department / Program name]


                    ──────────────────────


                    THESIS / INTERNSHIP REPORT
               submitted in partial fulfillment of the
                    [Degree, e.g.: Bachelor's in Business]


                    ──────────────────────


                    [THESIS TITLE]
              [Optional subtitle in italics]


                    ──────────────────────


                    Submitted by: [First name LAST NAME]

                    University supervisor: [First name LAST NAME, Title]
                    Company supervisor: [First name LAST NAME, Position]
                    Host company: [Company name]

                    Academic year: [20XX-20XX]

                    [Defense date if known]
```

### Variants by level

**High school (work experience):** Simplify. Name, first name, class, school,
company, placement dates, supervisor name.

**BTS:** Add the candidate number if required, the session (e.g. "Session 2026"),
the name of the examination.

**Research Master's:** Add the research laboratory name, the note
"under the supervision of [Director]", the exact program name.

## 3. Acknowledgements

### Rules

- 1 page maximum
- Order: university supervisor, company supervisor, company team,
  close ones (optional, brief)
- Tone: sincere and restrained. No superlatives ("extraordinary", "incredible")
- Name people by their title and full name
- Never thank "Claude" or an AI

### Template

```
ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS

I would first like to thank [First name LAST NAME], [position],
for [specific contribution: supervision, availability, advice on a particular aspect].

I also thank [First name LAST NAME], my company supervisor at [Name],
for [specific contribution].

My thanks also go to the entire [name of department/service] team
for [their welcome / their help with a specific task / the time they gave me].

Finally, I thank [close ones, optional] for [specific support].
```

## 4. Standard table of contents

### Conventions

- "Table of Contents" can appear at the start (summary of sections) or end (detailed, with page numbers) of the document.
- In practice, provide a detailed table of contents with page numbers.
- Numbering: I, II, III for main sections; A, B, C for sub-sections;
  OR 1, 1.1, 1.2, 2, 2.1... (numeric style, more common in science/management)

### Template (numeric style)

```
TABLE OF CONTENTS

Acknowledgements .................................................. i
List of abbreviations ............................................. ii
List of figures and tables ........................................ iii

Introduction ...................................................... 1

1. [Title of Section 1] ........................................... 4
   1.1. [Sub-section] ............................................. 4
   1.2. [Sub-section] ............................................. 8
   1.3. [Sub-section] ............................................. 12

2. [Title of Section 2] ........................................... 16
   2.1. [Sub-section] ............................................. 16
   2.2. [Sub-section] ............................................. 21
   2.3. [Sub-section] ............................................. 25

3. [Title of Section 3] ........................................... 29
   3.1. [Sub-section] ............................................. 29
   3.2. [Sub-section] ............................................. 33

Conclusion ........................................................ 37
Bibliography ...................................................... 39
Appendices ........................................................ 42
```

## 6. Bibliography: templates by standard

### APA 7: Complete example

```
BIBLIOGRAPHY

Autissier, D., Johnson, K., & Moutot, J.-M. (2022). Change management:
    Key concepts. Dunod.

Bharadwaj, A., El Sawy, O. A., Pavlou, P. A., & Venkatraman, N. (2013). Digital
    business strategy: Toward a next generation of insights. MIS Quarterly, 37(2),
    471-482. https://doi.org/10.25300/MISQ/2013/37:2.3

Department of Finance. (2023). Practical guide to accounting records.
    https://www.finance.example.gov/practical-guide

Vial, G. (2019). Understanding digital transformation: A review and a research
    agenda. The Journal of Strategic Information Systems, 28(2), 118-144.
    https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jsis.2019.01.003
```

### French citation standard: Complete example

*Note: the French citation standard uses footnotes with specific formatting conventions. Chicago Notes-Bibliography is the closest international equivalent.*

```
BIBLIOGRAPHY

BOOKS
AUTISSIER (David), JOHNSON (Kevin), MOUTOT (Jean-Michel), Change management:
    Key concepts, Paris, Dunod, 2022, 320 p.

ARTICLES
VIAL (Gregory), "Understanding digital transformation: A review and a research
    agenda", The Journal of Strategic Information Systems, vol. 28, no. 2, 2019,
    p. 118-144.

OFFICIAL TEXTS
[Applicable legislation with full citation]

ONLINE SOURCES
[Author], "[Title]", www.example.com, accessed [DD/MM/YYYY].
```

## 7. Appendices: conventions

### Rules

- Numbered: Appendix 1, Appendix 2... or Appendix A, Appendix B...
- Each appendix has a descriptive title
- Each appendix is referenced in the text: "(see Appendix 3)"
- Do not put in appendices what should be in the text
  (key results go in the body, raw data go in appendices)

### Typical appendix content

| Type of thesis | Common appendices |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Professional thesis | Org chart, interview guide, transcripts (extracts), screenshots of tools, raw data tables |
| Internship report | Org chart, schedule, working documents, photos of the site |
| Research thesis | Interview guide, full questionnaire, detailed statistical tables, thematic coding |
| Essay | Rarely appendices (except documents provided with the prompt) |

### Template

```
APPENDICES

Appendix 1: Company Organisational Chart ........................ 43
Appendix 2: Semi-structured Interview Guide ..................... 44
Appendix 3: Interview Transcript No. 1 (extracts) .............. 46
Appendix 4: VBA Macro Screenshot ............................... 50
Appendix 5: Processing Time Summary Table ...................... 51
```

## 8. Abstract

Often required in Master's 1 and 2. Placed after the cover page, before the table of contents.

### Template

```
ABSTRACT

[Thesis title]

This thesis addresses [topic in 1 sentence]. The central research question is:
[research question]. [1-2 sentences on the methodology]. [1-2 sentences on the
main results]. [1 sentence on the recommendations or contribution].

Keywords: [5-7 keywords separated by commas]
```

Length: 150-300 words (unless otherwise instructed).

## 9. Academic typography guidelines

### Font and page layout

| Element | Convention |
|---------|-----------|
| Body text font | Times New Roman 12pt OR Garamond 12pt OR Calibri 11pt |
| Line spacing | 1.5 (thesis) or 2 (PhD, if required) |
| Margins | 2.5 cm all round (left at 3 cm if binding) |
| Alignment | Justified |
| Pagination | Bottom of page, centered. Not on the cover page. |
| Numbering | Preliminary pages in Roman numerals (i, ii, iii), body in Arabic numerals |
| Footnotes | 10pt font, single spacing |

### Headings

| Level | Typical format |
|--------|---------------|
| Section heading (I, II, III) | Bold, 16pt, uppercase or small caps |
| Sub-section heading (1.1, 1.2) | Bold, 14pt |
| Section heading (1.1.1) | Bold, 12pt |
| No more than 3 levels | Beyond that, restructure the outline |

### Typography conventions

| Rule | Correct example |
|-------|----------------|
| Space before colon in languages that require it | Follow your institution's language style guide |
| Quotation marks | Use the convention appropriate to your language ("..." for English) |
| Ellipsis | … (single character) not ... (3 separate periods) |
| Numbers under 10 | Spell out: "three people" not "3 people" (follow style guide) |
| Percentages | 45% (no space in English; "45 %" in French, follow your institution's standard) |
