## 1. High school (Year 10 → Year 13)

### Types of work expected
- Essay (philosophy, social sciences, history-geography, literature)
- Text commentary / structured commentary
- Document study
- Internship report (work experience)
- Grand Oral (Year 13, French-specific final oral exam)
- Extended Project (TPE) / specific project

### Level of expectation
- Structured plan (introduction/body/conclusion), not necessarily problem-driven in Year 10
- Sources: textbooks, documents provided by the teacher, some independent research
- Style: clear, accessible-formal sentences, no jargon
- Essay length: 2-4 handwritten pages (Year 10), 4-6 pages (Year 13)
- No formal bibliography expected (except for extended projects)
- Emphasis on method (knowing how to organize an argument) over depth

### Common pitfalls at this level
- Paraphrasing documents without analyzing them
- Confusing "summarizing" with "arguing"
- List-based plan (ideas with no connecting thread)
- Introduction that asks no question
- Conclusion that repeats the introduction

### How to help
- Explain the method step by step, with examples drawn from the prompt
- Provide model opening sentences ("This document highlights...", "One observes that...")
- Check understanding of the prompt BEFORE allowing writing to begin
- Encourage the use of a structured rough draft

## 2. BTS / DUT (2-year vocational degree)

### Types of work expected
- Internship report (often a significant weighting)
- Case studies
- Professional projects
- Program-specific files (E4/E6 files for BTS, depending on specialty)

### Level of expectation
- Detailed description of tasks and the company
- Beginnings of analysis: not just describing, but explaining why and how
- Sources: company documents, course notes, a few external sources
- Internship report length: 20-30 pages excluding appendices
- Careful presentation: properly formatted cover page, table of contents, pagination
- Relevant appendices (no padding)

### Common pitfalls
- Report too descriptive (catalogue of tasks with no analysis)
- No link to the training program
- Appendices not referenced in the text
- Company section too long relative to the tasks section

## 3. BUT (3-year vocational bachelor's, French-specific degree)

*Note: BUT (Bachelor Universitaire de Technologie) is a French vocational degree delivered over 3 years at a University Institute of Technology (IUT). It combines academic and professional training, often with a work-study component. SAÉ (Situations d'Apprentissage et d'Évaluation) are compulsory group project assessments specific to this degree.*

### Types of work expected
- Internship report / work-study report each year
- SAÉ (group project assessments, French-specific, mandatory)
- Professional thesis in Year 3 (depending on specialty)
- Competency portfolio

### Level of expectation
- Research question expected from Year 2 (mandatory in Year 3)
- Explicit link to the BUT competency framework
- Sources: 8-15 references, textbooks and basic articles
- Methodology: simple but present (interviews, observation, analysis of internal data)
- Year 3 thesis length: 30-50 pages
- Critical analysis expected: not just "I did X" but "X worked because..."
- Concrete recommendations for the company

### BUT GEA / Accounting specifics
- Accounting standards cited precisely (PCG, IFRS, CGI, French-specific norms)
- Real figures expected (anonymized if necessary)
- Tools named (SAGE, Cegid, Excel, VBA...)
- Processes described rigorously (workflow, steps, controls)

### Common pitfalls
- Research question too broad ("How to improve accounting?")
- Theoretical section disconnected from the field
- Unrealistic recommendations (proposing a €100k ERP to a 5-person firm)
- Forgetting to link tasks to the competency framework

## 4. Bachelor's (Years 1-3)

### Types of work expected
- Essays (frequent in Years 1-2)
- Presentations and group assignments
- Mini-dissertation or dossier (Year 3)
- Internship report (if a placement is included)
- Case commentary (law), case studies (management), analysis papers (SSH)

### Evolution Year 1 → Year 3

| | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main expectation | Understand and recall | Analyze and argue | Synthesize and critique |
| Sources | Textbooks, course notes | Textbooks + a few articles | Articles + primary sources |
| Dossier length | 5-10 pages | 10-15 pages | 15-25 pages |
| Research question | Guided by the instructor | Semi-autonomous | Autonomous |
| Style | Accessible-formal | Formal | Academic |

### Common pitfalls
- Year 1: not understanding the difference between summarizing and analyzing
- Year 2: poorly integrated citations (pasted blocks of text without analysis)
- Year 3: research question too vague, outline that does not hold over 20 pages

## 5. Master's 1

### Types of work expected
- Master's 1 thesis (professional or research depending on the program)
- Internship / work-study reports
- In-depth case studies
- Group work with professional deliverable

### Level of expectation
- Argued research question anchored in the literature
- Structured literature review (15-25 sources)
- Methodology described and justified
- Analysis with confrontation of theory and field
- Thesis length: 40-60 pages
- Bibliography meeting the required standard (APA or institution standard)
- High writing quality
- Ability to formulate honest limitations

### What differentiates it from BUT/Year 3
- The "why" weighs as much as the "what" and the "how"
- The student must show an argued personal position
- Sources must be academic (not just websites)
- Methodology is no longer optional: it is assessed

---

## 6. Master's 2 professional

### Types of work expected
- Professional thesis (central piece)
- Assignment / project report
- Presentation before a professional panel

### Level of expectation
- Research question rooted in the field, illuminated by theory
- Solid literature review (20-30 sources)
- Rigorous methodology with justification for each choice
- In-depth analysis of results
- Realistic, costed recommendations where possible
- Critical discussion (strengths, limitations, scope)
- Length: 50-80 pages
- The panel expects work at a "near-professional" level, not a school exercise

### What differentiates it from Master's 1
- Intellectual autonomy is total
- The work must be "useful": applicable, transferable
- The position is clear: the student knows what they are defending
- Limitations are identified BEFORE the panel points them out

---

## 7. Master's 2 research

### Types of work expected
- Research thesis (60-100+ pages)
- Research article (in some programs)
- Oral communication at conference level

### Level of expectation
- Research question arising from a gap identified in the literature
- Critical and exhaustive literature review (40-80+ sources)
- Explicit and justified theoretical framework
- Epistemological positioning (positivist, interpretivist, constructivist...)
- Reproducible methodology with biases discussed
- Results presented with scientific caution ("the data suggest that...")
- Discussion confronting existing work
- Identified contribution to knowledge
- Impersonal style ("we" of modesty or passive constructions)

### What differentiates it from Master's 2 professional
- Theoretical contribution takes priority over practical utility
- The work should (in theory) be publishable or serve as a basis for a PhD
- Methodological rigor is assessed at the same level as beginning doctoral work
- Concepts are defined precisely; authors are discussed (not just cited)
