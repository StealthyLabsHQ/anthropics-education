# Reference: Anti-generic: 20 before/after pairs

This file is a reformulation catalogue. Each pair shows how to transform a hollow phrase
into a phrase that creates a difference. Use as a proofreading filter.

---

## Category 1: Quality declarations without proof

**1.**
❌ "I am known for my rigor and organizational skills."
✅ "At [company], I simultaneously managed [X client files] under deadline pressure, which taught me to prioritize and document every step so nothing was missed."
> Rule: every quality must be shown, not declared.

**2.**
❌ "I am dynamic and motivated by new challenges."
✅ "Facing a volume of bank reconciliations that was exploding at month-end, I chose to automate the process in VBA rather than extend working hours. This is the type of problem I'm looking to solve at a larger scale."
> Rule: show dynamism through a decision taken, not an adjective.

**3.**
❌ "I am a curious person who loves to learn."
✅ "Outside of my coursework, I completed the [name] certification on [platform] to master [tool], because I felt it was missing from my profile for the applications I was targeting."
> Rule: curiosity is proven by what you did without being required to.

---

## Category 2: Empty superlatives and praise of the target

**4.**
❌ "The quality of your teaching and the reputation of your institution convinced me."
✅ "The [exact name] module in the [title] Master's, focused on [specific content], directly addresses the gap I identified in my practice: I can read a balance sheet, but I can't yet design the IS architecture that produces it."
> Rule: replace praise with a precise connection between a program element and a real need.

**5.**
❌ "Your company is a sector leader and I wish to grow here."
✅ "The [recent project name] you led on [topic], which I discovered through [source], illustrates exactly the type of challenge I want to learn to address."
> Rule: show you've done research with a specific, recent example.

**6.**
❌ "Your program is recognized for its excellence."
✅ "The placement rate in MOA roles and the presence of [business partner or lab] in your network correspond precisely to the trajectory I'm targeting."
> Rule: excellence must translate into a measurable fact or a named element.

---

## Category 3: Sentences focused on what you receive

**7.**
❌ "This Master's will allow me to acquire the skills needed to succeed in this field."
✅ "My firm experience will allow me to approach the IS project management modules with an operational perspective that purely technical profiles don't necessarily have."
> Rule: §3 must first answer "what do I bring?" before "what do I receive?".

**8.**
❌ "I am convinced this opportunity will be beneficial for my professional development."
✅ "Joining this program would allow me to turn an empirical dual competency into a structured methodology, while offering future employers a profile that can speak equally to both the IT department and the CFO."
> Rule: frame the benefit in terms of value created, not abstract personal growth.

**9.**
❌ "I am looking to thrive in a stimulating environment."
✅ "I am looking for an environment where analytical rigor and the ability to model processes are valued as much as mastery of technical tools."
> Rule: describe the sought environment with precise criteria, not adjectives.

---

## Category 4: Weak verbs and passive formulations

**10.**
❌ "I had the opportunity to participate in various projects."
✅ "I designed and deployed [tool/process] for [objective], in coordination with [type of stakeholder]."
> Rule: "participate in" = vague contribution. Name the action and the result.

**11.**
❌ "I was involved in client file management."
✅ "I managed the follow-up of [X] clients on [VAT returns / reconciliations / tax filings], handling client communication alone during the manager's absences."
> Rule: "involved" erases the real level of responsibility. Name what you did independently.

**12.**
❌ "I was able to gain experience in this field."
✅ "Over [duration] of apprenticeship, I handled [type of files] and resolved [concrete problem], giving me a field perspective that coursework alone could not have provided."
> Rule: acquired experience only has value if proven by what it produced.

---

## Category 5: Industry generalities

**13.**
❌ "The finance sector is undergoing digital transformation."
✅ "The widespread adoption of BI and automation tools in accounting firms is transforming the junior accountant role in ways I hadn't anticipated when I started my apprenticeship, and that transformation is precisely what I want to learn to drive."
> Rule: if you mention a trend, connect it to a personally lived experience.

**14.**
❌ "IT careers offer many future prospects."
✅ Remove. This sentence contributes nothing. Replace it with the candidate's precise professional project.
> Rule: any industry generality can be replaced by a personal projection.

---

## Category 6: Closing formulas and outdated phrases

**15.**
❌ "Yours faithfully, / Sincerely yours,"
✅ "I remain available for an interview at your convenience and thank you for the attention given to my application."
> Rule: archaic formal closings feel distant in academic or startup contexts. Keep only for very formal contexts (major banks, public administrations).

**16.**
❌ "Hoping my application will receive your attention."
✅ "I would be delighted to present my background in person and discuss how my experience can contribute to [program/team]."
> Rule: conclude actively, not with a pleading passive.

---

## Category 7: Weak opening

**17.**
❌ "I am writing to apply for the position of [X]."
✅ Remove "I am writing to": it positions the candidate as a timid petitioner. Start directly with the differentiating angle or the connection with the target.
> Rule: the opening must create tension or a connection, not announce the sending of a document.

**18.**
❌ "Currently in [program], I am looking to join your Master's to deepen my knowledge."
✅ "[Observation or finding from field experience]: that is what I understood while [context]. It is precisely to go further in that direction that I am applying for the [title] Master's at [university]."
> Rule: start with a finding or a tension, not an administrative description.

---

## Category 8: Universal sentences (copy-pasteable into any letter)

**19.**
❌ "My background has allowed me to develop varied and transferable skills."
✅ "My [program] background combined with [X months] of apprenticeship in [sector] gave me a dual lens (that of the numbers and that of the system that produces them) which I rarely find in profiles from a single track."
> Rule: "varied and transferable" says nothing. Name the skills AND their unique convergence point.

**20.**
❌ "I am convinced my profile matches your expectations."
✅ Remove or reframe by showing the match: "The [skill 1] + [skill 2] combination I have developed corresponds precisely to the profiles described in [program / job description], particularly on [specific aspect]."
> Rule: never claim the match, demonstrate it.
