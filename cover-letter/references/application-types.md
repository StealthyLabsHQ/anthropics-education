# Reference: Application Types: specifics and examples

## 1. Permanent / Fixed-Term Employment

### Objective
Convince a recruiter that the candidate can deliver immediate and lasting value.

### Key points
- Precisely target the listing's tasks and respond to each one (without bullet lists)
- Highlight quantifiable results: "reduced by 30%", "managed a portfolio of X clients"
- Show stability and desire to be part of the company's project
- Adapt formality to the sector (banking/finance = very formal, startup = more direct)

### Template paragraph: experience
> "During my X years at [company], I managed [task] for a portfolio of
> [X clients / X files], which developed an analytical rigor and ability
> to work under deadline pressure, skills directly transferable to the target role."

---

## 2. Apprenticeship (work-study contract)

### Objective
Show the candidate understands the dual school/company logic and will be operational quickly.

### Key points
- Explain how the apprenticeship fits into a clear professional project
- Highlight prior experience (internships, summer jobs, academic projects)
- Show capacity for quick adaptation and learning
- Mention the school and program: the recruiter is also choosing the training

### Template paragraph: professional project
> "While pursuing a [degree] in work-study at [school], I am looking to put into practice
> the theoretical [field] curriculum within a structure like [company],
> recognized for [specific value/project]. This experience would allow me to consolidate
> my dual competency in [field 1] and [field 2], in line with my goal
> of moving toward [type of role]."

---

## 3. Internship

### Objective
Show motivation, curiosity, and capacity to contribute despite limited experience.

### Key points
- Highlight the program and what it brings theoretically
- Emphasize the desire to both learn AND contribute (not just "learn")
- Specify availability dates and internship duration
- If first internship: highlight academic projects, volunteer work, cross-disciplinary skills

### Template paragraph: contribution despite limited experience
> "Although my professional experience is still developing, my university projects
> in [field] (notably [concrete project]) have familiarized me with [tool/method].
> I want to put these foundations to work for your team, while enriching my practice
> through your projects."

---

## 4. Master's / School (university application)

### Objective
Demonstrate the coherence of the background, maturity of the professional project, and knowledge of the program.

### Key points
- Show logical progression: bachelor's → Master's → target careers
- Demonstrate knowledge of program specifics (modules, labs, partnerships)
- Articulate acquired AND skills to develop (humility + ambition)
- For hybrid profiles (e.g., accounting + tech): highlight the complementarity as an asset

### Template paragraph: hybrid background coherence
> "My [current program] background gave me solid skills in
> [field 1], which I supplemented with concrete practice in [field 2] at
> [institution]. This dual expertise, rare in the sector, corresponds precisely to
> the profiles sought by the [exact title] program, oriented toward [program axis]."

### Template paragraph: knowledge of the program
> "The [axis or module name] in [Master's X] at [university], combined with
> partnerships with [companies / labs mentioned], directly addresses my ambition
> to [precise career goal]. The pedagogy through apprenticeship/projects/real-case
> learning matches how I progress."

---

## 5. Parcoursup (French university application platform: specific constraints)

### Technical constraints
- 1500 characters maximum (unless stated otherwise on the program card)
- No name/surname in the text
- No date, no header, no salutation
- Format: plain text, no bullet points or headings
- Structure: intro (1 sentence), development, conclusion (1 sentence)

### Recommended writing order
1. Read the program's "national and/or supplementary requirements" on Parcoursup
2. Identify 2-3 skills or traits that directly respond to them
3. Write the development around those elements (experience + coursework)
4. Show knowledge of the program (open day, curriculum, careers)
5. Conclude with the professional project

### Template paragraph: Parcoursup intro
> "A student in [current program], I have developed a strong interest in [field]
> through [concrete experience/project], which led me to apply for the [exact
> program title] at [institution]."

### Adapted vocabulary (reminder)
- "student" not "pupil"
- Exact title: "BUT Informatique" not "IUT informatique", "Professional Bachelor's" not its abbreviation
- Avoid visible AI generation: overly smooth phrasing, forced synonyms, lack of singularity

## 6. Speculative Application

### Objective
Create a need where no listing has been published, by showing detailed knowledge of the company.

### Key points
- Specify what type of role is sought (not "anything")
- Show the candidate has done serious research on the structure
- Identify a latent need or strategic direction of the company
- Call directly to action: exploratory meeting, phone call

### Template paragraph: company knowledge + identified need
> "Following [company]'s development (notably [recent project / news /
> observed strategic direction]), I have identified strong synergies with my profile
> in [field]. With [X years/months] of experience in [key skill], I am reaching out
> to explore a role oriented toward [type of task]."

---

## Common Errors to Fix

| Error | Fix |
|---|---|
| "I am dynamic and motivated" | Replace with an example that proves it |
| Repeating CV dates and titles | Tell what the CV doesn't say |
| Generic salutation "Dear Sir/Madam" | Find the recruiter's or admission coordinator's name |
| Same letter for 5 different applications | Personalize §1 and §3 at minimum |
| Neglected spelling / syntax | Always proofread, use a spell checker |
| Visible AI (artificial synonyms, smooth sentences) | Inject concrete personal details |
| Too long (>1 page) | Remove generalities, keep examples |
| Closing with archaic formula without context | Adapt the closing to the letter's tone |
